Fall 2023 - “Modern Methods in Particle Physics” (Master’s/PhD, 27 lectures, still in development)
A rapid introduction to Quantum Field Theory, culminating in lectures on Effective Field Theories and the Standard Model Effective Field Theory. The course develops QFT through the path integral formulation focusing on developing calculational techniques necessary for particle physics research. The notes were primarily developed following the textbooks by Sterman, Srednicki, and Schwartz.

Fall 2022 - “Modern Methods in Particle Physics” (Master’s/PhD, approx 13 Lectures)
The course covered:
1) The SM, fields, symmetries, Lagrangian, Rules, Equations of Motion
2) Phenomenology of the Z-pole at LEP, tree level comparison with data, 2+ loop comparison with data (using parameterized results)
3) Effective Field Theories, Fermi theory, top-down and bottom-up EFTs
4) The Standard Model Effective Field Theory, deriving the Warsaw basis
5) Phenomenology in the SMEFT
6) Loops and EFTs, introduction to Chiral Perturbation Theory
